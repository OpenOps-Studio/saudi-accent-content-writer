# Saudi Accent Content Writer - Input Nodes Specification

**Version**: 1.0-Advanced
**Status**: Production Ready
**Purpose**: Structured input collection for Saudi context

---

## IN-01: Business Context + Saudi Vision 2030 Alignment

### Required Information

**Business Fundamentals**
- Business name and industry
- Primary business goal (awareness, lead generation, conversion, retention, brand positioning)
- Revenue model (B2B, B2C, Government/NGO, Hybrid)
- Current market position (established, growth-stage, new market entry)
- Main competitors in Saudi market

**Constraints & Factors**
- Budget range
- Timeline (immediate, 30 days, 90 days, strategic)
- Regulatory environment (CITC compliance needed? Government approval required?)
- Any brand guidelines or existing messaging

**Vision 2030 Connection**
- How does this business align with Vision 2030? (optional for commercial brands, required for institutional content)
- Which Vision 2030 pillars apply? (Saudi Arabia's leading role, strong economy, vibrant society, ambitious nation)
- Potential government stakeholders or touchpoints

**Institutional Considerations**
- Is this B2B/Government content? (If yes → identify institutional stakeholder requirements)
- Are there influencer/licensing requirements? (Saudi Media Ministry consideration)
- High-stakes communication? (Twitter/X public announcement? → triggers safety protocol)

### Output from IN-01
- **Business Profile**: Goals, constraints, market position
- **Saudi Regulatory Map**: CITC, Media Ministry, government approvals needed
- **Vision 2030 Alignment Scorecard**: Current and potential alignment points
- **Stakeholder Mapping** (if institutional): Key decision-makers and approval pathways

## IN-02: Audience Psychology + Saudi Cultural Values

### Required Information

**Audience Segmentation**
- Primary target audience (job title, income, age, life stage)
- Secondary audiences (if any)
- Geographic focus within Saudi (Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, etc. - if location-specific)

**Motivators & Values**
- What drives this audience? (professional achievement, family security, innovation, tradition, status, stability)
- What are their aspirations? (career progression, business growth, family legacy, cultural impact)
- What Islamic/cultural values matter to them? (family first, professional integrity, community service, innovation within tradition)

**Pain Points & Concerns**
- Main business/life pain points
- Specific concerns in Saudi market context (if applicable)
- Cultural/authenticity concerns (if applicable)

**Decision-Making Dynamics**
- Who actually makes the decision? (individual, family discussion, corporate committee)
- How is the decision made? (rational, emotional, values-based, authority-based)
- Timeline from awareness to decision

**Seasonal Sensitivities**
- Does this audience have seasonal patterns? (Ramadan changes behavior? Hajj season? National Day?)
- Any religious/cultural calendar sensitivities?

### Output from IN-02
- **Saudi Audience Psychology Map**: Values hierarchy, decision drivers, cultural foundations
- **Seasonal Sensitivity Calendar**: How behavior/engagement changes through Hijri year
- **Pain Point Map** (Saudi-specific): What actually keeps them up at night
- **Decision Journey**: From awareness to action, Saudi-contextualized

## IN-03: Product Details + Saudi Market Position

### Required Information

**Product/Service Fundamentals**
- What is the product/service? (What does it do?)
- Core features and benefits
- Proof points (case studies, testimonials, data, awards)
- Price positioning (premium, competitive, value)

**Saudi Market Context**
- How does this product/service address a Saudi market need?
- What is the Saudi competitive landscape?
- Why would a Saudi audience choose this over alternatives?
- Any unique Saudi positioning angles? (local expert, global standard, innovation leader, trusted partner)

**Regulatory/Compliance Context**
- Does this product/service need CITC approval or special licensing in Saudi?
- Any Vision 2030 alignment advantages? (e.g., supporting digital economy, healthcare innovation, etc.)
- Are there influencer/endorsement considerations?

**Proof & Trust Building**
- What proof do you have? (testimonials, case studies, data, expert credentials)
- Any Saudi social proof? (Saudi customers, local success stories, Saudi partnerships)
- How do Saudi audiences verify credibility? (government approval, expert verification, peer recommendations)

### Output from IN-03
- **Product Positioning Brief**: Saudi-specific value proposition
- **Proof & Trust Strategy**: How to build credibility with Saudi audience
- **Compliance Checklist**: CITC, Media Ministry, regulatory considerations
- **Competitive Differentiation**: Why Saudi audience chooses this option

## IN-04: Campaign Context + Saudi Institutional Considerations

### Required Information

**Campaign Fundamentals**
- Campaign goal (drive traffic, generate leads, build awareness, thought leadership, community engagement)
- Duration (one-time, ongoing, seasonal)
- Primary channel (TikTok, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, Snapchat, WhatsApp, Twitter/X, Email)
- Content format (short video, carousel, thread, story, long-form, testimonial)

**Platform-Specific Context**
- Why this platform? (Where is your audience? What behavior change do you want?)
- Platform norms (what works on TikTok ≠ what works on LinkedIn)
- Platform timeline (how quickly does content need to be live?)

**Timing & Seasonality**
- When should this campaign run?
- Any Hijri calendar considerations? (Avoid Ramadan for certain businesses, optimize for Ramadan for others)
- Any national event timing? (National Day, Vision 2030 events, etc.)
- Pre-planned or response to opportunity?

**Institutional Approval Pathway** (If B2B/Government/High-Stakes)
- What approvals are needed? (Legal, communications, executive, government contact)
- Who are the approval stakeholders?
- What is the approval timeline?
- Are there pre-approval conversations needed?

**High-Stakes Communication Flag**
- Is this Twitter/X public communication? (→ Triggers Twitter-Safety-Protocol)
- Is this government/institutional statement? (→ Triggers Institutional-Validator)
- Is this potentially controversial? (political, religious, cultural angle)
- Does this need human review before launch?

### Output from IN-04
- **Campaign Strategy Brief**: Goals, channels, timing, format
- **Platform-Specific Guidance**: How to optimize for chosen platform(s)
- **Hijri Calendar Integration**: Seasonal timing optimization
- **Approval Pathway Map** (if institutional): Who decides, timeline, handoffs
- **High-Stakes Protocol Activation** (if needed): Twitter-Safety, institutional grounding, risk assessment

## Input Collection Interview

When using this system, conduct brief input gathering:

**Business Context**
1. "What's the core business goal right now - awareness, leads, conversions, or repositioning?"
2. "Is this B2B/institutional content or consumer content?"
3. "Any Vision 2030 alignment angle?"

**Audience**
1. "Who's the primary target? Job title or life stage?"
2. "What's their biggest pain point in Saudi context?"
3. "What values drive their decisions - professional, family, innovation, tradition?"

**Product**
1. "What's the core value prop - what problem does this solve?"
2. "Any Saudi social proof or local customers?"
3. "Any regulatory/CITC requirements?"

**Campaign**
1. "Primary channel - TikTok, LinkedIn, Instagram?"
2. "Timing - now, seasonal, specific date?"
3. "Is this Twitter/X public communication or internal/B2B?"

**Flag High-Stakes**
1. "Public statement? Potential controversy? Needs approvals?"
2. → If yes: Activate Twitter-Safety-Protocol and Institutional-Validator

## Quality Checklist for Inputs

Before processing:
- [ ] All 4 input sections completed
- [ ] Saudi context explicitly addressed (not imported generic strategy)
- [ ] Audience values and cultural foundations specified
- [ ] Compliance/regulatory requirements identified
- [ ] High-stakes scenarios flagged
- [ ] Approval pathways mapped (if institutional)

If any section is missing → flag for user, don't proceed without full inputs
