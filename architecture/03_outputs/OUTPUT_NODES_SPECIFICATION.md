## OUT-01: Saudi Content Playbook

### Purpose
Comprehensive execution plan for launching Saudi-optimized content across channels

### Contents

**Executive Summary**
- Campaign objective
- Target audience (Saudi segment + values hierarchy)
- Primary channels and tactics
- Expected engagement performance (% above baseline)
- Timeline and key milestones
- Success metrics

**Strategic Positioning**
- Market positioning (vs. competitors in Saudi)
- Vision 2030 alignment (if institutional)
- Institutional stakeholder map (if B2B)
- Key messaging pillars (max 3)
- Brand voice guidelines (tone, accent tier, cultural touchpoints)

**Channel-by-Channel Strategy**
For each primary channel (TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.):
- Platform-specific objective
- Optimal accent tier (Saudi/Gulf/MSA) with rationale
- Content format (short video, carousel, thread, etc.)
- Posting frequency and timing
- Engagement benchmarks (% above Saudi average)
- Content calendar (specific posts, posting schedule)

**Accent Tier Guidance Per Platform**
- **TikTok**: Saudi dialect optimized, casual, trending sounds
- **Instagram**: Saudi/Gulf mix, visual-focused, community engagement
- **LinkedIn**: Gulf/MSA blend, professional thought-leadership
- **YouTube**: Platform-specific optimized (varies by content type)
- **WhatsApp**: Saudi dialect for community engagement
- **Twitter/X**: Platform-specific (with safety protocol if public)

**Seasonal Campaign Calendar** (Hijri Year + National Events)
- Ramadan messaging strategy (dates, content angle, engagement expectations)
- Hajj season approach (dates, messaging tone, expected engagement)
- Eid messaging (Eid al-Fitr + Eid al-Adha content plans)
- National Day strategy (September 23, Vision 2030 alignment)
- Regular season baseline messaging

**Launch Checklist with Compliance Verification**
- [ ] Accent authenticity verified (1-5 score: ___/5)
- [ ] CITC compliance confirmed
- [ ] Media Ministry requirements checked (if influencer marketing)
- [ ] Vision 2030 alignment verified (if institutional)
- [ ] Institutional approval pathways mapped (if B2B)
- [ ] Twitter-Safety-Protocol completed (if public communication)
- [ ] Reputational risk assessment done
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity reviewed
- [ ] All stakeholder approvals obtained
- [ ] Performance tracking dashboard configured

**Performance Tracking Template**
- KPI definitions (engagement rate, conversion, sentiment, compliance)
- Measurement methodology (how to track each KPI)
- Reporting frequency (daily? weekly? monthly?)
- Stakeholder reporting structure (who gets which reports)
- Dashboard access and login information

**Escalation & Iteration Plan**
- What metrics trigger optimization?
- When to pause and reassess?
- Process for seasonal adjustments
- Approval process for major content changes
- Human review gates (especially for Twitter/X public communication)

## OUT-02: Saudi Content Variants

### Purpose
Multiple content versions optimized for testing and platform-specific deployment

### Includes

**Accent Tier Variants** (3 versions)
- **Saudi Dialect Version**: Most casual, highest trust with youth audience
  - Sample: "وش السالفة؟ الحل الجديد يغير كل شيء في ١٠٠%"
  - Platform: TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram Stories
  - Authenticity Score: _/5
  
- **Gulf Arabic Version**: Professional yet warm, broader regional appeal
  - Sample: "شلونك! لدينا حل جديد يساعدك في الموضوع"
  - Platform: YouTube, Instagram Feed, LinkedIn (sometimes)
  - Authenticity Score: _/5
  
- **MSA Version**: Formal, authoritative, institutional
  - Sample: "نسعد بتقديم الحل المتطور الذي يعالج التحديات"
  - Platform: LinkedIn, Official announcements, Government communication
  - Authenticity Score: _/5

**Platform-Specific Adaptations**
- TikTok version (15-60 seconds, trending sounds, Saudi slang)
- Instagram version (carousel, reel, or story format, visual-focused)
- LinkedIn version (professional article or thought-leadership post)
- YouTube version (short or full-length, educational angle)
- Email version (campaign ready, click-through optimized)
- WhatsApp version (community-focused, shareable)

**Content Type Variants** (Testing different angles)
- Curiosity-driven version ("Want to know X?")
- Benefit-driven version ("Save 20 hours/week")
- Values-driven version ("Professional excellence, Saudi values")
- Authority-driven version ("Used by X Saudi institutions")
- Story-driven version ("This Saudi founder's journey...")

**Seasonal Content Variants**
- **Ramadan Version**: Spiritual tone, family values, evening timing
- **Hajj Season Version**: Spiritual significance, community focus, lighter promotion
- **Eid Version**: Celebration tone, gift-giving, family gathering angle
- **National Day Version**: Pride, Vision 2030 alignment, Saudi identity
- **Regular Season Version**: Professional focus, achievement angle

**A/B Testing Variants**
- Conservative vs. Cultural-touchpoint emphasis
- Direct CTA vs. Soft-invitation CTA
- Long-form vs. Snackable format
- Social proof-heavy vs. Feature-focused

### Output Format

Each variant includes:
1. **Full Content** (headline + body copy + CTA)
2. **Accent Tier**: Specific tier used
3. **Platform Optimization**: Platform-specific format
4. **Authenticity Score**: 1-5 rating
5. **Expected Performance**: Engagement rate prediction vs. baseline
6. **Testing Notes**: What this variant tests vs. others
7. **Launch Status**: Ready to launch / Needs review / Flagged for compliance

## OUT-04: Performance Dashboard Specification

**Measurement Methodology**

**Sentiment & Audience Response Dashboard**

**Seasonal Performance Dashboard**

## OUT-05: Saudi Brand Voice Guidelines

### Purpose
Living document for maintaining consistent, authentic Saudi brand communication

### Contents

**Accent & Tone Standards**
- **When to use Saudi Dialect** (Tier 1):
  - Platforms: TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram Stories
  - Audience: Youth, social followers, relatable positioning
  - Tone: Friendly, casual, confident
  - Example Phrases: "وش السالفة؟", "يلا نروح", "صراحة"
  
- **When to use Gulf Arabic** (Tier 2):
  - Platforms: YouTube, Instagram Feed, Email
  - Audience: Professional yet broad appeal, regional campaigns
  - Tone: Professional warmth, inclusive
  - Example Phrases: "شلونك?", "كيفك انت في...", "يسعدك الله"
  
- **When to use MSA** (Tier 3):
  - Platforms: LinkedIn, Government communication, Press releases
  - Audience: Formal, institutional, authority-based
  - Tone: Formal, authoritative, respect-based
  - Example Phrases: "يسرنا أن نعلن", "نود إطلاعكم", "نشرّفنا"

**Platform-Specific Voice Variations**
- **TikTok Voice**: Native to platform, trending language, Saudi slang acceptable
- **Instagram Voice**: Visual-first, community-engaged, emoji-appropriate
- **LinkedIn Voice**: Thought leadership, professional achievement, expert positioning
- **YouTube Voice**: Educational or entertaining, depending on content type
- **WhatsApp Voice**: Community-focused, personal, relationship-building

**Cultural Touchpoint Library**

**Professional Pride References**:
- "طموح سعودي" (Saudi ambition)
- "تميز واحترافية" (excellence and professionalism)
- "قيادة تحتية" (leadership credentials)
- "رؤية المملكة" (Kingdom's vision)

**Family & Community Values**:
- "أسرتك أولاً" (your family first)
- "قيم عائلية" (family values)
- "ثقة المجتمع" (community trust)
- "مسؤولية اجتماعية" (social responsibility)

**Islamic Principles References**:
- "أمانة وعدل" (integrity and justice)
- "أخلاق إسلامية" (Islamic ethics)
- "خدمة المجتمع" (community service)
- "نية طيبة" (good intention)

**Saudi Identity References**:
- "الرؤية السعودية" (Saudi Vision)
- "اقتصاد إعاد" (resilient economy)
- "مجتمع حيوي" (vibrant society)
- "القيادة والريادة" (leadership and innovation)

**"What NOT to do" - Cultural Cringe Guardrails**
- ✗ Forced slang or expressions that don't sound natural
- ✗ Stereotypical cultural references
- ✗ Mocking family or Islamic values
- ✗ Disrespect to government or national institutions
- ✗ Casual code-switching (mixing Saudi dialect with formal MSA awkwardly)
- ✗ Unauthentic attempts at cultural connection
- ✗ Treating Saudi dialect as "less than" Gulf or MSA

**Vision 2030 Alignment Principles** (If institutional)
- How to authentically connect to transformation narrative
- How to support government priorities
- Language that signals government alignment
- Thought leadership positioning within Vision 2030 context
- How to avoid performative alignment

**Institutional Communication Requirements** (If B2B/Government)
- Formal approval language requirements
- Stakeholder acknowledgment protocols
- Government entity reference standards
- Compliance language integration
- Institutional hierarchy respect in communication

**Authenticity Verification Checklist**
Before any major content launch:
- [ ] Read aloud - sounds natural?
- [ ] Saudi native speaker would recognize and approve?
- [ ] No forced expressions or stilted language?
- [ ] Cultural references authentic (not stereotyping)?
- [ ] Accent tier matches context?
- [ ] Values integrated authentically (not performative)?
- [ ] Institutional positioning (if applicable) is strategically sound?

## OUT-06: Twitter/X Safety Protocol** (If Public Communication)

### Purpose
Pre-approval framework for public communication on Twitter/X

### Includes

**Institutional Stakeholder Research Summary**
- Key institutional players who might respond/react
- Government entities with interest in topic
- Competitor stakeholders who might respond
- Influencer allies and potential critics
- Media entities who might amplify
- Public opinion landscape on topic

**Risk Assessment by Category**

**Political Risk**:
- Credibility risk (does this strengthen or weaken brand positioning?)
- Government relationship risk (any sensitivity to current administration?)
- Vision 2030 alignment risk (how might government respond?)
- Mitigation language options

**Religious Risk**:
- Islamic values risk (could this offend religious standards?)
- Hajj/Umrah sensitivity (if applicable to topic)
- Sectarian consideration (avoiding sectarian divides)
- Mitigation language options

**Reputational Risk**:
- Peer response (how will professional community react?)
- Customer perception (could this damage brand trust?)
- Media amplification (could negative interpretation spread?)
- Competitor response (could competitors weaponize this?)
- Mitigation language options

**Pre-Launch Review Checklist**
- [ ] Institutional stakeholder map complete
- [ ] Risk assessment finalized (political/religious/reputational)
- [ ] All risks rated Low/Medium/High
- [ ] Mitigating language options developed
- [ ] Response templates prepared for high-risk scenarios
- [ ] Editorial calendar has human review gate
- [ ] Final approval obtained from leadership

**Approved Language Variations**
For each main message, provide:
- Primary phrasing (optimal authenticity)
- Conservative phrasing (lower risk)
- Diplomatic phrasing (if politically sensitive)
- Mitigating language (if potential backlash)

**Response Templates for Likely Criticism**
- Template for political criticism
- Template for religious concern
- Template for competitive attack
- Template for misunderstanding/misinterpretation
- Template for media inquiry

**Editorial Calendar with Approval Gates**
- Pre-draft review (leadership review concept)
- Draft review (before thread written)
- Final review (before scheduling)
- Post-launch monitoring (first 24 hours)
- Escalation protocols (if unexpected reaction)

## OUT-07: Seasonal Campaign Templates

### Purpose
Pre-built frameworks for optimizing messaging during Hijri calendar seasons and national events

### Includes

**Ramadan Campaign Template**
- **Dates**: [SPECIFIC HIJRI DATES]
- **Engagement Expectation**: +200-300% vs. baseline
- **Messaging Angle**: Spiritual + family values + community focus
- **Tone**: Reverent, warm, inclusive
- **Accent Tier**: Saudi dialect (social) or Gulf Arabic (professional)
- **Content Types**: Stories, values-based messaging, community connection
- **Content Calendar**: 30 days of Ramadan content framework
- **Example Headlines** (Ramadan-specific):
  - "رمضان كريم - كيف نقدم الأفضل لأهلنا" (Ramadan Kareem - how to give our best to our families)
  - "في رمضان، نتذكر قيمنا المشتركة" (In Ramadan, we remember our shared values)
- **CTA Approach**: Invitation to community/participation (not aggressive selling)
- **Cultural Touchpoints**: Family gathering, spiritual reflection, generosity, community service
- **What to Avoid**: Aggressive promotion, food content, secular messaging, disrespect to spiritual nature

**Hajj Season Template**
- **Dates**: [SPECIFIC HIJRI DATES]
- **Engagement Expectation**: Elevated but specific pattern (many in Mecca, reduced business activity)
- **Messaging Angle**: Spiritual pilgrimage, sacred journey, community values
- **Tone**: Respectful, reflective, communal
- **Accent Tier**: Gulf Arabic (professional) or MSA (formal)
- **Content Types**: Inspiration, community connection, testimonial
- **Content Calendar**: Hajj season messaging framework
- **Example Headlines** (Hajj-specific):
  - "الحج والعمرة - رحلة روحية وقيمة مجتمعية" (Hajj and Umrah - spiritual journey and community value)
- **Engagement Strategy**: Less promotional, more inspirational/community-focused
- **Cultural Touchpoints**: Sacred pilgrimage, Islamic practice, community gathering, spiritual renewal
- **What to Avoid**: Commercial promotion, secular content during sacred season

**Eid Al-Fitr Campaign Template**
- **Dates**: [SPECIFIC DATES - Ramadan + 1]
- **Engagement Expectation**: +150-200% vs. baseline
- **Messaging Angle**: Celebration, achievement (fasting completed), family, generosity
- **Tone**: Joyful, warm, inclusive
- **Accent Tier**: Saudi dialect (celebratory) or Gulf Arabic
- **Content Types**: Celebration content, gift ideas, family messaging, gratitude
- **Example Headlines**:
  - "عيد سعيد - احتفل مع عائلتك وأحبائك" (Happy Eid - celebrate with family and loved ones)
- **CTA Approach**: Celebration invitation, gift suggestions, community connection
- **Cultural Touchpoints**: Family celebration, generosity, gift-giving, community joy
- **What to Avoid**: Business-as-usual messaging, missing celebratory tone

**Eid Al-Adha Campaign Template**
- **Dates**: [SPECIFIC DATES - 10th of Dhu al-Hijjah]

- **Messaging Angle**: Sacrifice, family gathering, legacy, tradition
- **Tone**: Reverent, family-focused, grateful
- **Accent Tier**: Gulf Arabic (to reflect sacrifice's regional significance)
- **Content Types**: Family messaging, community connection, values-driven
- **Example Headlines**:
  - "عيد الأضحى - أسرة واحدة تحتفل بتقاليدنا الغالية" (Eid Al-Adha - one family celebrating our precious traditions)

**National Day Campaign Template** (September 23)

- **Tone**: Proud, aspirational, visionary

- **Vision 2030 Integration**: How brand supports national transformation
- **Cultural Touchpoints**: Saudi identity, national pride, leadership, innovation
