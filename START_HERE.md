## The 3-Tier Dialect Architecture

### Tier 1: Saudi Dialect (Most Casual)
**When**: Social media, relatable brand voice, youth marketing
**Platforms**: TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram Stories, WhatsApp
**Examples**: "وش السالفة؟", "يلا نروح", "صراحة"
**Authenticity**: Highest with youth audience, builds maximum trust
**Tone**: Friendly, conversational, confident
**Example Content**: "وش السالفة؟ الحل الجديد يغير اللعبة بالكامل"

### Tier 2: Gulf Arabic (Professional + Warm)
**When**: Cross-GCC campaigns, professional content, broader appeal
**Platforms**: YouTube, Instagram Feed, Email, LinkedIn (sometimes)
**Examples**: "شلونك؟", "كيفك انت في...", "يسعدك الله"
**Authenticity**: Professional credibility, regional appeal
**Tone**: Professional warmth, inclusive, respectful
**Example Content**: "شلونك في السوق السعودي؟ عندنا الحل اللي تحتاجه"

### Tier 3: MSA (Formal/Institutional)
**When**: Government communication, official statements, formal announcements
**Platforms**: LinkedIn, Government communication, Press releases
**Examples**: "يسرنا أن نعلن", "نود إطلاعكم", "نشرّفنا"
**Authenticity**: Formal authority and credibility
**Tone**: Formal, authoritative, institutional
**Example Content**: "يسرنا تقديم الحل المتقدم الذي يعالج تحديات السوق السعودي"

## Key Principles (Read These First!)

### 1. **Authenticity Above All**
If accent feels forced or unnatural, the system flags it. Never compromise authenticity for speed.

### 2. **Institutional Navigation is Strategic**
In Saudi market, understanding who decides and how they decide is as important as understanding audience psychology.

### 3. **Vision 2030 is Opportunity**
Align brand narrative with government transformation goals → you become part of national story.

### 4. **Professional Pride is Primary Motivator**
Saudi professionals are driven by professional excellence and leadership opportunity.

### 5. **Family Values are Foundation**
Family security and respect inform decision-making across all segments.

### 6. **Islamic Values are Real, Not Performative**
Authentically integrate Islamic principles, not as box-checking.

### 7. **Seasonal Timing Matters More**
Hijri calendar, Ramadan, Hajj dramatically shift engagement patterns.

### 8. **Compliance is Non-Negotiable**
CITC requirements, Media Ministry licensing, content sensitivity are not optional.

### 9. **Test Saudi-Specific Variations**
What works globally doesn't work in Saudi. Always test accent variants.

### 10. **Measure What Matters**
Track engagement, sentiment, compliance status, not just clicks.

## Common Questions

**Q: I need content in 30 minutes. Can this system deliver?**
A: Yes. Use the Express mode (30 min). You'll get ready-to-launch content variants with authenticity verification and basic testing plan.

**Q: How does this differ from using a general copywriter?**
A: This system is Saudi-specialized with institutional grounding, regulatory compliance checking, seasonal optimization, and accent-switching logic built-in. A general copywriter wouldn't understand CITC requirements or Vision 2030 alignment.

**Q: What if we're in Ramadan? How does the system adjust?**
A: The Seasonal-Optimizer layer automatically detects timing and adjusts:
- Messaging tone shifts to spiritual + family values
- Engagement expectations increase 3x
- Content types adapt (less promotion, more values/community)
- Hijri calendar awareness throughout

**Q: We're doing a Twitter/X announcement. What's different?**
A: Twitter-Safety-Protocol activates automatically:
- Mandatory institutional stakeholder research
- Comprehensive risk assessment (political/religious/reputational)
- Mitigating language options development
- Response templates for likely criticism
- Full pre-launch review before posting

**Q: How do we measure if content is "authentically Saudi"?**
A: Three ways:
1. Authenticity Score (1-5): Regular verification by Saudi native speakers
2. Engagement Metrics: Saudi audience engagement 40-50% above global average
3. Sentiment Analysis: 80%+ positive sentiment in comments
4. Manual Review: No "cultural cringe" incidents from audience

**Q: Can we use this for other Arabic dialects (Egyptian, Levantine, etc.)?**
A: This system is Saudi-specialized. For other dialects, consult the EgyptianDialectWriter or LevantineLocalizer agents. The principles can adapt, but Saudi-specific institutional grounding, Vision 2030 alignment, and CITC compliance won't apply.

**Q: Who actually approves content before launch?**
A: Depends on content type:
- Social content: Marketing/content team
- Institutional content: Legal + executive + stakeholder approval
- Twitter/X public statement: Leadership + compliance review
- The system maps the approval pathway for you
